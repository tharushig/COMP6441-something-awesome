Curious about history? You've come to the right place!

Locks were originally knots made out of rope but it is unsure as to who made the first mechanical lock.

A wooden pin tumbler lock was made in Ancient Eygypt over 6000 years ago but it was not very secure.

So let's take a look into the slice of history and unlock some information about locks.

Joseph Bramah Safety Lock

In the 1770s, the 'perfect lock' otherwise known as the Joseph Bramah Safety Lock was created by Joseph Bramah in England.

The lock required a key to be opened and consisted of complex layers between the key and the deadbolt.

Bramah had so much confidence in his lock that he offered 200 guineas ($30000) to anyone who was able to pick the lock.

Chubb Detector Lock

In response to Bramah's contest, the British government wanted a lock that created an alert upon attempted opening. This resulted in Chubb Detector Lock being created by Jeremiah Chubb.

The key to the lock didn't work if the tumbler was lifted too high, making it unable to open.

This lock was also thought of as being 'perfectly secure' as no one was able to pick it

With the Chubb and Bramah locks, there was a period of 'perfect security' lasting from the 1770s until 1851 when the locks were beaten.

The End Of a 'Perfectly Secure' Era

A.C Hobbs was an American locksmith who sold his own locks and taught bank managers how to pick their locks.

In 1851, Hobbs was able to pick Chubb's lock in 25 minutes and Brahmah's lock in 52 hours which unfortunately marked the end of 'perfect security'.

Ever since then, 'perfect security' could never be attained again. But that didn't stop the development of locks.

Although the pin tumbler lock already existed, Linus Yale Jr mass-produced these and patented it for a variety of uses such as banks, doors and padlocks from 1851 onwards.

Modern security relies heavily on technology and electrical systems, however, mechanical locks are still used for doors, padlocks and so on.
